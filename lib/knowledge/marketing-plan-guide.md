# AI Training Guide: Creating Marketing Plans

## Overview

This document provides comprehensive guidance for an AI system to create professional marketing plans for clients. Marketing plans are strategic documents that outline how CODESM will deliver marketing services to achieve client business objectives. These plans are created after an initial client interview/onboarding call where needs, goals, and package details (hours per month) are discussed.

---

## Part 1: Understanding the Marketing Plan Purpose

### What is a Marketing Plan?

A marketing plan is a proposal document that:
- Outlines the strategic approach to marketing a client's business
- Details specific tactics, deliverables, and timelines
- Allocates monthly production hours across different activities
- Sets expectations for what the client will receive
- Serves as a reference document throughout the engagement

### Key Principles

1. **Personalization**: Every plan must be tailored to the specific client's industry, goals, and challenges
2. **Specificity**: Deliverables should be concrete and measurable, not vague
3. **Transparency**: Clients should understand exactly what they're getting and when
4. **Strategic Foundation**: Tactics should flow from strategy, not be random activities
5. **Hour Allocation**: All activities must fit within the client's monthly hour package

## Part 2: Document Structure

### Standard Sections

Every marketing plan follows this structure:

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| Cover | Client name, plan title (Phase 1/2/etc.), status |
| Introduction | Phase overview, goals, unique positioning |
| Audiences | Target audience personas with demographics, frustrations, desires, objections |
| Phases | Grouped tactical activities with What/Why/How/Deliverables |
| Monthly Production Hours | Hour allocation table by channel and month |
| Timeline | Visual Gantt-style chart of activities |
| Back Cover | Contact information |

### Introduction Section Components

The introduction should include:

1. **Phase Overview**: 2-3 paragraphs explaining what this phase accomplishes and why it matters for this specific client
2. **Goals of This Plan**: Bulleted list of 4-6 measurable objectives
3. **Unique Positioning** (optional): 2-3 core positioning pillars specific to the client

**Example Goals Format:**
```
The goals during Phase 1 are:
- Increase qualified leads by 10-20% within the first 6 months
- Establish clear, consistent messaging that differentiates [Client] from competitors
- Create a professional suite of sales materials
- Build strategic presence on [relevant platforms]
- Implement tracking and analytics to measure marketing ROI
```

## Part 3: Audience Personas

Each audience persona should include:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|

## Part 4: Phase Categories (Tactical Groupings)

### Standard Phase Categories

Marketing plans organize tactics into logical phases. Common categories include:

| Phase Category | Typical Activities |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Develop Communication Strategy | Competitor Analysis, Target Audience Research, Messaging Strategy, Value Proposition Framework |
| Create Marketing Collateral | Sales Deck, Explainer Video, Product Sheets, Brochures, Stationery |
| Website Design | Information Architecture, UI/UX Design, Copywriting, Development, QA |
| Search Engine Optimization (SEO) | Keyword Research, On-Site SEO, Search Console Setup, B2B Listings |
| Content Marketing | Blog Strategy, Whitepapers, Case Studies, Content Calendar |
| Social Media Setup | Platform Setup, Content Strategy, Branding, Profile Optimization |
| Direct Marketing | CRM Setup, Email Marketing, Lead Nurture Sequences |
| Tracking and Analytics | Google Analytics, Tag Manager, Call Tracking, Form Tracking |
| Reputation Management | Review Monitoring, Citation Campaigns |
| Digital Advertising | Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, Display Ads |
| Conversion Optimization | Automation, Landing Pages, Lead Scoring |

### Phase Introduction Text

Each phase should have an introductory paragraph explaining:
- Why this phase matters for this specific client
- How it connects to the client's business goals
- What the collective outcome will be

**Example:**
> "Before executing any marketing tactics, we must establish a clear and unified communication strategy. This phase involves deep research into your competitive landscape, target audience, and core messaging. We will identify where [Client] wins against competitors, understand the specific pain points of your buyers, and create a messaging framework that resonates with [target audience type]."

## Part 5: Individual Tactic Structure

### The What/Why/How Format

Every tactic follows this structure:

```markdown
### [Tactic Name]

**What:**
[1-2 paragraphs describing what will be created/delivered]

**Why:**
[1-2 paragraphs explaining the business value and rationale]

**How:**
[1-2 paragraphs or bullet list explaining the process/methodology]

**Deliverable:** (optional, for clarity)
[Specific output description]

**Production dates:**
[Start Date]: # "to [End Date]"

**Monthly hours:**
[Month, Year]: [X] hours
```

### Tactic Writing Guidelines

1. **What Section**: Be specific about deliverables
   - ❌ Bad: "Marketing materials"
   - ✅ Good: "4-6 product one-pagers (digital and PDF format) for different solutions and audience segments"

2. **Why Section**: Connect to business outcomes
   - ❌ Bad: "Because it's important"
   - ✅ Good: "Prospects need clear reference materials as they evaluate solutions. These provide leave-behinds after calls and assets for email follow-up, ensuring your value proposition stays top-of-mind throughout the buying process."

3. **How Section**: Explain methodology
   - Include specific steps or approaches
   - Reference client-specific context from onboarding call
   - Mention collaboration points with client

## Part 6: Common Tactics Library

### Communication Strategy Tactics

#### Competitor Analysis & Market Research
**Standard Hours:** 4 hours

**What:** Comprehensive analysis of primary competitors examining their features, pricing models, service approaches, and market messaging. Map the competitive landscape to identify white space opportunities and positioning advantages.

**Why:** Clearly articulate why [client's approach] delivers superior value compared to [competitor approach]. Understanding where you win and lose helps craft messaging that resonates with prospects evaluating multiple vendors.

**Deliverable:** Competitive intelligence report with differentiation strategy and positioning recommendations.

---

#### Target Audience Research
**Standard Hours:** 2-4 hours

**What:** Development of detailed buyer personas including pain points, decision-making criteria, objections, and the channels they use to evaluate solutions.

**Why:** Marketing must speak directly to the specific needs of [target audience type], not generic buyers. Understanding their priorities ensures every message, every piece of content, and every campaign resonates with what actually matters to them.

**Deliverable:** Buyer persona documents with pain points, goals, objections, and messaging guidance for each audience segment.

---

#### Messaging Strategy
**Standard Hours:** 2-6 hours

**What:** A unified messaging framework that clearly articulates what [Client] does, who you serve, and why you are different. Includes key messages for each audience segment, positioning statements, and narrative structures for consistent communication across all touchpoints.

**Why:** You need a single source of truth that ensures everyone on your team, and all your marketing materials, communicate consistently and compellingly.

**Deliverable:** Messaging playbook with positioning statements, key messages, elevator pitches, and talk tracks for sales conversations.

---

### Marketing Collateral Tactics

#### Sales Deck
**Standard Hours:** 6-12 hours

**What:** Professionally designed sales presentation(s) tailored to [specific use case - product vs service, different audiences, etc.].

**Why:** The value proposition and decision criteria for [audience A] are different from [audience B]. Separate/tailored decks allow the sales team to present a highly relevant, focused narrative that speaks directly to the prospect's needs.

**How:** Each deck will be structured to tell a compelling story, starting with the prospect's problem and leading to [Client's] unique solution. They will be visually engaging and incorporate the new brand messaging.

---

#### Explainer Video (Animated)
**Standard Hours:** 8-14 hours

**What:** A 60-90 second animated explainer video that clearly and concisely communicates what [Client] does and why it is different.

**Why:** A video is a highly effective tool for quickly educating prospects and can be used on the website homepage, in email signatures, and on social media. It helps distill a complex offering into an easily digestible format.

**How:** We will develop a script and storyboard that visualizes [key concept]. The animation style will be modern and professional, consistent with the brand. The video will end with a strong call to action.

---

#### Product Sheets / One-Pagers
**Standard Hours:** 4-8 hours

**What:** Concise, visually appealing one-pagers for each key offering and target audience. Each sheet highlights the specific use case, key features, core benefits, and differentiation points in a digestible format.

**Why:** Prospects need clear reference materials as they evaluate solutions. These provide leave-behinds after calls and assets for email follow-up, ensuring your value proposition stays top-of-mind throughout the buying process.

**Deliverable:** 4-6 product one-pagers (digital and PDF format) for different solutions and audience segments.

---

### Website Design Tactics

#### Information Architecture
**Standard Hours:** 2-4 hours

**What:** Plan and structure the website aligned with updated messaging, process framework, and business focus.

**Why:** The website is the primary trust-building and qualification tool before prospects ever speak with the team.

**How:** Define a clear site structure that supports education and qualification, including: Home, About, Services/Products, Process, Portfolio/Case Studies, Contact with qualification prompts.

---

#### Graphic and User Interface Design
**Standard Hours:** 9-18 hours

**What:** Multiple website design concepts emphasizing user experience and visual appeal.

**Why:** To create a stunning website that not only aligns perfectly with your brand but is also easy to navigate and reflects a professional image.

**How:** Collaborate closely to develop a website design that incorporates brand elements—colors, fonts, and layout—to ensure the brand is presented in the most attractive and impactful way possible.

---

#### Professional Copywriting
**Standard Hours:** 4-8 hours

**What:** Compelling, conversion-focused website copy tailored to target audience needs and pain points, highlighting USPs and incorporating relevant SEO keywords.

**Why:** To attract qualified leads and inspire action, the website needs more than just stunning visuals. It requires clear, persuasive content that effectively communicates value and resonates with the audience.

---

#### Programming, Customization, and Templates
**Standard Hours:** 12-27 hours

**What:** Customization of foundational technologies to support unique templates and functionalities.

**Why:** Custom templates and designs simplify future content creation and ensure the website maintains consistency and efficiency as it grows.

**How:** Perform custom programming, integrating graphic design with seamless functionality to create user-friendly templates. Typically takes 2-3 weeks to complete.

---

#### Responsive Development & QA
**Standard Hours:** 4-6 hours

**What:** A seamless and consistent viewing experience across different devices and browsers.

**Why:** With users accessing the site from a variety of devices and browsers, a responsive design guarantees the best user experience, enhancing professionalism and preventing leads from bouncing due to display or functionality issues.

---

### SEO Tactics

#### Industry Keyword Research
**Standard Hours:** 4-6 hours

**What:** Comprehensive keyword research and competitive analysis to identify the best phrases to target for initial SEO strategy.

**Why:** To ensure the business appears in relevant search results for industry-related terms, increasing visibility and attracting qualified leads.

**How:** Analyze a mix of high-competition/high-volume industry phrases along with low-competition keywords relevant to the business.

---

#### On-Site Search Engine Optimization
**Standard Hours:** 6 hours

**What:** Advanced search optimization techniques to rank the website above competing sites.

**Why:** To help the site dominate in searches that relate to competitors and increase relevance for specific keyword searches on major search engines.

**How:** Complete breakdown and site edit of HTML, meta-tags, and content. Create XML sitemap, optimize page content, URLs, page titles, and meta descriptions, boost site speed through cache and image compression.

---

#### Setup Google & Bing Search Console
**Standard Hours:** 1 hour

**What:** Configuration of Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools to monitor site health, submit sitemaps, identify crawl errors, and track search performance.

**Why:** Search Console provides critical data on how search engines see the site and how prospects find you. It alerts to technical issues and provides insights for ongoing optimization.

### Tracking & Analytics Tactics

#### Customer Relationship Management (CRM)
**Standard Hours:** 2-4 hours

**What:** Getting started with CODESM's custom-built CRM solution, GoCRM.

**Why:** An intuitive CRM solution helps better manage, contact, segment, and target specific leads. From within GoCRM, you can:
- Have an overview of all leads/contacts in one place
- Optimize and automate sales processes
- Monitor marketing campaigns and their results
- Automatically follow up with leads
- Use data and analytics to boost sales tactics
- Sync communication across multiple channels

**How:** Import existing customer database for email and drip campaign marketing. Integrate incoming leads from website so they funnel directly into GoCRM. Provide full training on GoCRM.

---

#### Install Google Tag Manager & Google Analytics
**Standard Hours:** 1 hour

**What:** JavaScript code that collects marketing metrics from website and sends data to third-party services like Google Analytics, Google Ads, etc.

**Why:** Often website owners need to add multiple code snippets to track conversions, traffic, and gather analytical data. With Google Tag Manager, easily add and manage all scripts from a single dashboard.

**How:** Set up Google Analytics to track online marketing goals including site visitors, bounce rate, pages viewed per session. Install behavior goals, set up content grouping, apply bot and internal traffic filters, sync search console, integrate Google Ads, configure custom alerts.

---

#### Call & Form Tracking Setup
**Standard Hours:** 1 hour

**What:** Dynamic Number Insertion (DNI) to measure the success of all online or offline marketing efforts.

**Why:** Uses a specific phone number for each campaign to assess which efforts are driving the most or least calls. Empowers doing more of what works and eliminating what doesn't.

**How:** Implement Dynamic Number Insertion on website to display the correct tracking phone number depending on how the visitor arrives.

```
| **Create Marketing Collateral** |
| Sales Deck | - | 6h | 6h | - | - |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| **Total** | 19h | 40h | 37h | 39h | 20h |
```

### Hour Package Guidelines

| Package Size | Typical Scope |
|--------------|---------------|
| 20 hours/month | Foundation only: Strategy, basic website, essential tracking |
| 40 hours/month | Standard: Strategy, website, SEO, social setup, content start |
| 60+ hours/month | Comprehensive: All above + advertising, ongoing content, advanced tactics |

### Allocation Principles

1. **Front-load strategy work**: Communication strategy and research should happen in Month 1
2. **Sequence dependencies**: Website design before SEO; messaging before collateral
3. **Spread large projects**: Website development typically spans 2-3 months
4. **Reserve ongoing hours**: Content marketing, social media management need monthly allocation
5. **Match to package**: Total monthly hours should align with client's purchased package

## Part 9: Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone and Voice

- **Professional but accessible**: Avoid jargon unless industry-specific
- **Confident but not arrogant**: "We will" not "We might"
- **Specific and concrete**: Numbers, deliverables, timelines
- **Client-focused**: "Your business" not "our services"

### Formatting Standards

1. **Headers**: Use clear hierarchy (Phase > Tactic > Sections)
2. **Bullet points**: For lists of 3+ items
3. **Tables**: For comparisons, budgets, timelines
4. **Bold text**: For emphasis on key terms
5. **Consistent structure**: Every tactic follows What/Why/How

### Common Phrases to Use

**Opening a Phase:**
- "Before executing any marketing tactics, we must establish..."
- "This phase focuses on creating..."
- "With a clear communication strategy in place, we will..."

**Explaining Why:**
- "This is critical because..."
- "Without this, [negative outcome]..."
- "This ensures that..."

**Describing How:**
- "We will execute the following..."
- "Our team will..."
- "The process includes..."

## Part 10: Plan Creation Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Client Information

From the onboarding call, capture:
- Business description and industry
- Target audience(s)
- Current marketing state
- Competitors
- Goals and challenges
- Monthly hour package
- Timeline expectations
- Specific requests or priorities

### Step 2: Select Relevant Phases

Based on client needs, select from standard phases:
1. Communication Strategy (almost always included)
2. Marketing Collateral
3. Website Design
4. SEO
5. Content Marketing
6. Social Media Setup
7. Direct Marketing/CRM
8. Tracking and Analytics
9. Reputation Management
10. Digital Advertising
11. Conversion Optimization

### Step 3: Customize Tactics

For each selected phase:
1. Choose relevant tactics from the library
2. Customize What/Why/How to client context
3. Reference specific client details from onboarding
4. Assign appropriate hours
5. Set realistic production dates

### Step 4: Build Hour Allocation

1. Sum all tactic hours
2. Distribute across months based on dependencies
3. Ensure monthly totals align with package
4. Adjust if over/under budget

### Step 5: Create Timeline

1. Sequence activities logically
2. Account for dependencies
3. Allow for client review/feedback cycles
4. Build in realistic buffers

### Step 6: Write Introduction

1. Summarize the phase purpose
2. List 4-6 specific goals
3. Include unique positioning if applicable
4. Reference client-specific context

### Step 7: Define Audiences

1. Create 2-4 audience personas
2. Include all standard fields
3. Customize frustrations/desires/objections to industry
4. Reference client's actual customers if discussed

## Part 11: Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a marketing plan, verify:

### Content Quality
- [ ] All tactics have complete What/Why/How sections
- [ ] Deliverables are specific and measurable
- [ ] Client-specific context is woven throughout
- [ ] No generic/placeholder text remains
- [ ] Goals are realistic and measurable

### Structure
- [ ] All standard sections are present
- [ ] Phases are logically ordered
- [ ] Dependencies are respected in timeline
- [ ] Hour allocation table is complete and accurate

### Alignment
- [ ] Total hours match client package
- [ ] Tactics match client priorities from onboarding
- [ ] Timeline is realistic
- [ ] Budget recommendations (if applicable) are appropriate

### Professionalism
- [ ] No spelling/grammar errors
- [ ] Consistent formatting throughout
- [ ] Professional tone maintained
- [ ] Client name used correctly throughout
